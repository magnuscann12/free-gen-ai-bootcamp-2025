**Sentence Structure**

Sentence 1: + +
Sentence 2: + + +[subject][location][verb][object]

**Clues and Considerations**
- First sentence: The animal word goes in subject position. The place word goes before the verb.
- Second sentence: "You" is the subject. The thing you might have left is the object. The place word goes after the verb.
- For "leave" you need to decide which verb fits better: one means "to put/place", the other means "to forget".
- For "at the door": Chinese usually puts the location before the verb that describes where something is.
- Think about what particle shows a completed action in the second question. You’ll have to add that yourself.
- Remember to make it a question for the second sentence — that part isn’t in the vocab table.
